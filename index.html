A Comparison of Lexical Tokenization Methods

Honors research project. I compared several methods of tokenization in a simple sentiment analysis task.
Primarily, I wanted to compare morphological tokenization and Wordpiece tokenization, with the hypothesis that morphological tokenization would perform better.
I also looked at inserting tokens to indicate capitalization for non-case-sensitive tokenization methods.
The results were somewhat inconclusive. I think it was because the task is pretty simple; a more complex task (e.g. translation) could have better results.

Transfer Learning for Ionic Liquid Property Prediction

Research work from my time as a research assistant in the NRT-HDR: Graduate Traineeship on Advances in Materials Science Using Machine Learning.
This work built on the series of works Extensive Databases and Group Contribution QSPRs of Ionic Liquid Properties from Dr. Kamil Paduszynski.
These works modelled viscosity, surface tension, and density of ionic liquids from data on the functional groups present in those liquids.
We looked at how knowledge could be transferred from one model to another, with the goal of improving training time and required dataset size for modelling new properties.
We also looked at how pruning could be used to reduce the size of the model to increase explainability, and how pruning networks would affect the knowledge transfer.
This poster was designed by me and presented at the annual symposium for our research program.
